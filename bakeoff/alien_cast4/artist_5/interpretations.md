# Wedge and halo: cast interpretations

These are artist_5's readings of the nine beings. Each says what kind of
character the creature is, how it moves, and which design themes the drawing
leans on. The cast-level design hypothesis lives verbatim in the opening XML
comment of every SVG; this file is the creature-level companion to it.

## Humanoid

The humanoid is the cast's one true person, the everyman standing shoulder to
shoulder with machines and monsters. It reads as a confident, plucky scrapper:
even at rest it keeps a fist half-raised, the posture of someone always ready
to lend a hand or, if the moment calls for it, take a swing. It is not a hero
so much as a willing participant, the kind of character who volunteers first
and worries later.

Its movement is a full, energetic stride. Frame 1 stands akimbo, weight
settled and eyes forward; frame 2 throws the walk wide open, the arms and legs
swapping high and low so the silhouette clearly reads as a person mid-step. The
round skull sits over an angular wedge torso on purpose: a soft head on a hard
chassis, so the being feels both approachable and built to work.

## Flapper

The flapper is a large, sharp-eyed aerial hunter, and it wants you to know it.
It is imperious in the exact way a hawk riding a thermal is imperious, never
deigning to touch the ground, never conceding that ground is where it belongs.
The whole lower third of its canvas stays empty air, a deliberate declaration
that this creature has no feet and needs none.

At rest it holds its wings in a raised display V, alert and watchful, scanning.
In motion it commits to a full downstroke, the wings sweeping down and forward
into the least ambiguous flight pose available. The diamond head and beak keep
the read predatory and precise, so the silhouette is unmistakably a flier and
never a walker caught in an odd pose.

## Bonzoid

The bonzoid is a boisterous, manic little tank: pure Johnny 5 energy stuffed
into a chassis that was built for enthusiasm rather than precision. It rolls
everywhere it goes on a solid tread slab, arms flailing with far more
excitement than coordination, and it treats every situation as the best
situation it has ever been in.

Its single stalk-mounted sensor lens is the whole personality in one shape: one
big binocular eye taking in everything at once, wide and eager. The oversized
asymmetric arms swap high and low between frames to sell the over-energized
rolling motion, so the creature never looks still even when it is standing. The
solid tread base is a deliberate identity signal, holding it apart from the
mechtron's split posts in silhouette alone.

## Gollumer

The gollumer is a mournful, gentle slug-mass, Hutt-soft and low to the ground,
moving through the world by patient ooze rather than by will. It is heavy in a
sad way, a being that seems too tired and too weighed down to ever sit up
straight, content to watch the day pass from wherever gravity last left it.

It leans and settles asymmetrically, and that asymmetry flips side between
frames to carry the slow slide of the ooze. A single half-lidded, downcast eye
does all the emotional work; there is no gold accent anywhere on it, because a
mournful creature does not need bling to say what it is. Its low wide mound is a
different silhouette family from the cast's taller creatures, and its whole flat
underside meets the ground with no break, which is the read that keeps it apart
from the packer's daylight-under-the-belly hop.

## Spheroid

The spheroid is a small, startled UFO tripod, wide-eyed and endlessly curious,
permanently hovering a little above where it should be. Its three legs dangle
loose and useless beneath it, as if it forgot they do not actually reach the
ground and has decided not to worry about it. It is the most innocent thing in
the cast, a saucer that seems perpetually surprised to find itself airborne.

It bobs and drifts and never lands, never walks. Frame 1 lets the legs hang
straight; frame 2 swings them to one side like a pendulum, selling the float
without ever letting a foot touch down. The compact lens-and-dome saucer body
keeps it unmistakably distinct from the flapper: both never land, but one is
winged and this one is an orb with a single enormous startled eye.

## Leggite

The leggite is a sly, skittering standing centipede, tall and narrow and always
faintly furtive, as though it is about to dart out of frame the moment your
attention drifts. Its whole column undulates side to side as its many legs
carry it along, and that constant wave is the tell of a creature that trusts
speed and evasion over confrontation.

Its centerline zigzags left and right through seven segments to build the
undulating read. Frame 2 is the exact horizontal mirror of frame 1, every shape
reflected across the column, so the wave visibly travels the other direction
without redesigning the body at all. The many small legs fringing the column
run top to bottom, making the locomotion legible in pure silhouette.

## Mechtron

The mechtron is a heavy, deadpan machine, all rectilinear posture and stoic
patience. It carries real weight in everything it does and is in no hurry to
explain itself. Where the other machines in the cast are eager or nervous, this
one is simply immovable until it decides to move, and then it moves like
something that expects the ground to get out of its way.

It walks with genuine ponderous heft. Frame 1 stands as two straight posts with
a wide hole between them; frame 2 bends one leg forward into a two-segment
stomp while the other stays a rigid post, so the stride reads as slow and
loaded. A flat visor slit stands in for eyes, because a stoic machine does not
need to blink to be watching. Head and torso are fused into one slab, machine
geometry rather than human proportion, and the 72-unit hole between the posts is
the widest deliberate gap in the cast.

## Packer

The packer is a huge, dopey, cheerful mass of a creature that quite literally IS
its own cargo, hauled around on four comically tiny T-rex legs that have no
business supporting something that big. It is delighted by everything. It hops
where it is going like an overjoyed toad, and the gap between what it weighs and
what carries it is the whole joke of the design.

Its body is one enormous blob; the head is barely a bump on the top of it, down
to a small gold cargo-tag stuck on its forehead like a shipping label on a
parcel. There is real daylight under its belly between those stubby legs. Frame
1 plants all four tiny feet; frame 2 tucks every leg up mid-hop, opening the gap
even wider and making the frog-like bound unmistakable in silhouette.

## Mule

The mule is a quietly dependable, patient robotic beast of burden, built the way
a BigDog is built, and it wears no face because it does not need one to earn
your trust. The affection it invites is the mild, real affection you would have
for a favorite tractor or an old pickup truck: not a pet, a working machine that
always starts.

It plods forward at a steady, load-bearing pace. Its rigid rectangular chassis
rides on four thin jointed legs arranged in two close pairs, with real daylight
beneath the slab and a clear gap between the front and rear pair. Frame 1 stands
calm and vertical; frame 2 kicks the rear pair forward and the front pair back
into a plain working stride. Exposed pivot joints and canted rigid ears keep the
read industrial and mule-shaped at once, and a single gold cargo strap across
the chassis is the only ornament it allows itself.
