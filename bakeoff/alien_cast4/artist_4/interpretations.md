# Artist 4 cast interpretations

Persona: **The Expressionist**. I begin with the personality word, not
the silhouette. Emotional register shapes geometry. Rounded masses for
dopey or gentle creatures; angular edges for sharp or stoic ones; cascading
asymmetry for the sly and mournful.

Ground-contact order: I design the bottom edge before the rest of the body.
It is the cheapest identity signal at 18px and the axis that separates the
nine creatures most reliably in silhouette alone.

Face rule: oversized eyes using the light-circle-on-dark-pupil construction,
sized generously so the personality word reads at badge size. Frame 2 shows
maximum displacement of the creature's most personality-relevant feature.

---

## Humanoid

Confident, plucky - the everyman. The humanoid is wide-shouldered and
barrel-chested, planted with comfortable authority. Frame 1 is a relaxed
stand: arms hanging at a slight angle from the body, feet at shoulder width.
The geometry is bilateral and upright - confidence is symmetry and vertical
posture. Frame 2 is a clear walking stride with left leg forward and right
arm counterbalancing forward. The face carries a wide satisfied smile and
two large eyes that look directly forward. The gold accent is a chest badge -
the plucky worker's sign of belonging. Bottom edge: two heel pads with a
wide crotch gap that survives the halo at game size.

---

## Flapper

Sharp, alert, imperious. The flapper is a pterodactyl-boned glider with a
narrow pointed head, swept wings, and a forked tail. The body is a tight
diamond between the wing roots - the creature IS its wings. The head is an
upward-pointing triangle terminating in a small gold beak tip. Eyes are
narrow horizontal slits: alert and a little imperious, scanning the horizon
below. Frame 1 is a level glide with wings swept back. Frame 2 is an active
downstroke with both wings angled sharply upward, showing maximum wingspan
spread and tipping the head forward as the creature powers through air.
Bottom edge: nothing. Clear air beneath the tail fork in both frames.

---

## Bonzoid

Manic, boisterous. Johnny 5 energy distilled into geometry. The bonzoid is
a barrel-bodied machine with two oversized mechanical arms and a small excited
head perched on top. The arms are the story: in frame 1 both arms are raised
high in a Y-shape of pure enthusiasm. The tank tread base is a wide flat slab -
unbroken, corner to corner. Frame 2 shows the motion: right arm punches
dramatically downward and backward while the left arm drives forward and up,
a kinetic windmill spin that unmistakably communicates rolling energy. Face:
huge eyes with wide pupils and an open excited mouth. Bottom edge: one
unbroken tread slab - no gap, no split.

---

## Gollumer

Mournful, gentle. The gollumer is a two-lobed blob: a tall crown lobe on
the left that leans slightly leftward (the head mass) and a wider lower body
lobe spreading to the right. The whole underside is flat against the ground -
no gap, no limbs. The asymmetry is intentional: the gollumer leans as though
carrying weight it cannot put down. Face: two drooping-lidded eyes set low
on the crown lobe, asymmetrically spaced, looking downward. A small neutral
line for a mouth. Frame 2 shows the ooze: the crown squashes lower, the body
mass spreads wider and flatter. The creature elongates in the direction of
travel and its profile changes from a tall teardrop to a wide spreading
puddle. Bottom edge: complete flat contact, no gaps or protrusions.

---

## Spheroid

Startled, curious, wide-eyed. The spheroid is a War-of-the-Worlds flying
saucer: a wide disc body with three thin legs dangling below, never touching
the ground. The disc has a dome above and a slight underdome below for the
classic saucer profile. The three legs hang in an open tripod array. The
face is dominated by two enormous staring eyes - the personality word is
"startled" so the eyes take up most of the available face real estate. Frame
2 shows the float: the saucer drifts upward and the three legs swing into
new pendulum positions. The whole silhouette shifts - the saucer center rises
and the legs describe a different angle. Bottom edge: nothing. Three dangling
prongs that never reach the ground floor.

---

## Leggite

Sly, skittering. The leggite is a narrow vertical column - almost a spine -
fringed with three pairs of legs staggered up its height. The face at the top
is narrow and cunning: two tight horizontal eyes and a slight asymmetric smirk.
Asymmetry is built into the design at two levels: the column itself is very
slightly off-center (its center of mass tilts), and the leg heights are
staggered so left and right legs on the same segment are NOT at the same
vertical position. Frame 2 shows the slither: the column bends into a subtle
S-curve (upper segment shifts left, lower shifts right), and the leg positions
change to a rippling wave pattern that communicates many-legged travel. Bottom
edge: six leg tips, the column running all the way top to bottom.

---

## Mechtron

Stoic, deadpan. The mechtron is a heavy rectangular slab on two thick posts,
all hard right angles, no organic curves at all. The body is massive and wide.
Two short antennae stand on the top corners. The two leg posts have a wide gap
between them - the gap is the mechtron's most important identity signal and
is sized to survive the 28-unit halo expansion at game size. The face is a
single wide horizontal sensor bar with two circular sensor nodes: deadpan and
machine-like, no eyebrows, no expression. Frame 2 shows the ponderous heavy
step: the left leg post lifts 40 units clear of the ground, breaking the
bilateral symmetry and communicating exactly one deliberate mechanical stride.
Bottom edge: two thick rectangular posts with a large clear gap between them.

---

## Packer

Dopey, cheerful. The packer is a nearly spherical body - a big round dome -
balanced on four comically tiny T-rex stump legs. The body is the whole
creature; the legs are almost afterthoughts. Face: large round eyes that are
slightly close-together for the dopey read, and a wide open happy-arc mouth.
Frame 1 is the crouch: body low, four stumps planted, wide and comfortable.
Frame 2 is the hop: the whole body lifts cleanly off the ground by 40+ units,
all four legs tucked up beneath the mass. The silhouette change is dramatic -
the bottom edge of the dome clears the ground line entirely. Bottom edge in
frame 1: four tiny claw tips with clear belly space between them. Bottom edge
in frame 2: the dome's own lower arc, with tucked legs visible beneath.

---

## Mule

Quietly dependable, patient. The mule is a robotic BigDog-style quadruped
engineered for hauling. The body is a wide horizontal slab - a cargo platform.
The head has long ears and a forward-projecting muzzle clearly readable as a
mule profile. Four thin mechanical leg-columns stand beneath the slab with
real daylight visible between and beneath them. The legs are rectangular
and segmented for the industrial-machine read. No face badge - the mule
ships no face crop. Frame 1 is the standing load-bearing posture: all four
legs planted, slab level, ears vertical. Frame 2 is the plod: front-left and
rear-right legs lifted and stepped forward together (diagonal-pair gait),
breaking the resting symmetry and communicating slow deliberate movement.
Bottom edge: four leg column bottoms with daylight beneath the slab between
the pairs.
