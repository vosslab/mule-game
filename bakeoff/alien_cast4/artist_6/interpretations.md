# Artist 6: interpretations (round 4)

Lane: artist_6. Persona: THE HERALD.

## Design hypothesis (cast level)

Heraldic charges solved sprite legibility six centuries ago: a beast on a banner must be
identified across a battlefield, which is the same problem as an 18 px sprite. My vocabulary:
profile-first single-mass bodies; exaggerated attitude poses borrowed from blazonry (statant
at rest; passant, volant, glissant, salient in motion); deep contour notches instead of
interior detail; tapered limbs that end in emphatic points; one dominant diagonal axis in
every frame 2. Faces are light eye shapes with dark pupils under one heavy lid or brow line
that carries the personality word. I believe attitude, the tilt of the whole mass, reads at
18 px when anatomy does not.

## Humanoid

The standard-bearer: a confident, plucky scout who walks everywhere like the road is his.
Frame 1 is statant, feet planted wide, chin up, one fist on hip; frame 2 is passant,
mid-stride, front leg reaching, back heel peeled off the ground, lead arm swung high. The one
creature in the cast built on a person's plan, so I keep him almost symmetric and break it
with swagger.

Design themes: big head, short sturdy legs, mitten hands; an everyman badge, not a fashion
figure. Wide eyes under high brows: up for anything.

## Flapper

An imperious pterosaur that has never once landed and does not intend to start. Everything
below it is, in every sense, beneath it. Frame 1 soars, wings in a high shallow V, spearhead
skull scanning; frame 2 is the volant downstroke, wings snapped below the shoulder line, body
lunging, tail vane flicked.

Design themes: all triangles and crescents; the body stays skinny so the wing owns the
canvas; clear air under it in both frames. A narrow haughty lid over a bright eye; the gold
beak tip is the one jewel this noble permits itself.

## Bonzoid

A machine built by someone who confused enthusiasm with engineering: a turret torso riding
one fat tank tread, two oversized arms that never stop moving, no legs at all. It does
everything at 140 percent and announces it. Frame 1 stands on its tread with both arms up
mid-gesture, telling you the news; frame 2 rolls flat out, body pitched forward over the
tread, arms flung back like streamers, cleats shifted round the tread.

Design themes: the tread is a dark ground-hugging lozenge, the maintainer's Johnny 5 signal,
and everything above it is angular clatter. Two wide aperture eyes of unequal size: too
awake.

## Gollumer

A mournful Hutt, a soft lopsided hill of a being that ooze-walks on its own belly and
apologizes for the trail. Frame 1 slumps at rest, a heavy teardrop with a drooped head lobe;
frame 2 is glissant, the mass stretched forward and lower, front lip reaching, crest thrown
back, a wave moving through pudding.

Design themes: no break anywhere in the bottom edge, the whole underside is ground;
asymmetry is the identity. Heavy-lidded eyes set low, a small downturned mouth: mournful,
but kind about it.

## Spheroid

A startled War of the Worlds saucer, three legs dangling like pulled roots, floating and
mildly alarmed to be here. It drifts up to things, peers, recoils. Frame 1 hovers level,
legs hanging loose; frame 2 bobs up and heels over, legs swung trailing to one side, clear
air under it always.

Design themes: dome plus brim disc silhouette, thin knuckled tendril legs that never reach
the ground. One enormous ringed eye with a porthole pupil and raised lids: permanently
startled, incurably curious.

## Leggite

A standing centipede, sly as a pickpocket: a tall wavering column of segments fringed with
legs. It does not walk anywhere, it pours itself there. Frame 1 holds a gentle S with the
head hooked over like a question mark; frame 2 inverts and deepens the S, head craned round,
legs mid-ripple at alternating angles so the travel wave shows in silhouette.

Design themes: asymmetry throughout; the leg fringe is both the bottom edge and the side
signature. Narrow side-set eyes under a flat lid: watching you decide not to watch it.

## Mechtron

A shipping container that learned to walk and refuses to hurry. Two piston posts with a hole
of daylight between them, machine geometry with no human proportion: no arms, a slab, a
sensor brow. Frame 1 stands level, both posts planted; frame 2 swings one whole post forward
and tips the slab a few degrees over the planted side, mass visibly committed. Ponderous is
the point.

Design themes: everything at 90 degrees except the walk tilt; the only curves in the body
are the eye apertures. Two square pale eyes with flat half-drawn lids: deadpan, unimpressed,
on schedule.

## Packer

A dopey, cheerful cargo-beast: one huge rounded mass, because it IS its own cargo, held off
the ground by four tiny T-rex limbs. It hops like a toad and is delighted every single time.
Frame 1 squats sejant on all four little feet, a sliver of daylight under the belly, face
front; frame 2 is salient, mid-launch, mass tilted back, front limbs up, rear limbs driving.

Design themes: the mass is four fifths of the creature, the limbs comically small but
load-bearing. Round eyes set wide apart, an open grin: not one thought behind them, all of
it happy.

## Mule

Not a character, a colleague. A robotic literal mule on the BigDog plan: horizontal slab
body with a segmented cargo spine, long ears, a muzzle, a tail, so the silhouette says mule
while the construction says machine. Frame 1 stands square, ears up, waiting for the load;
frame 2 plods, diagonal legs advanced, head dropped a notch into the pull, tail swung, back
level because the load must not tip.

Design themes: exposed joints, rigid segments, four thin legs with real daylight beneath the
slab. No face, no badge; you know it by its outline and its gait, the way you know your own
tractor.
