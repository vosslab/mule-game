# Cast 4, artist 2 -- interpretations

## Persona and design hypothesis

Persona: the bold picture-book toymaker. Each creature is a chubby carved-wood
folk toy: at most six or seven fat rounded primitives, one dominant focal
feature per face, and honest asymmetry where the contract calls for it.
Personality lives in the eye SHAPE and pose more than in line work, so faces
run large and unfussy -- surprised orbs, sly slits, a single mournful lens.

Geometric vocabulary: fat rounded rectangles and generous ellipses, corners
rounded (`rx`) so the halo and ink strokes bloom into pleasing bumps rather
than sharp needles at 18-32 px. Limbs are stubby (packer, mule) or oversized
(bonzoid, leggite) -- never medium. Every bottom edge is designed first, on
purpose, because the ground contact is the cheapest identity signal a player
gets (contract rule).

Frame 2 is a big pose beat, not a subtle blink. Wings that were spread wide
tuck vertical; a body that was upright twists mid-stride; four feet that
were planted tuck up under a hopping belly; a leg-wave phase inverts. If the
silhouette outline does not visibly redraw, the frame is wasted.

Gold is a single warm dot per creature -- the thing the eye finds first. On
gollumer it is the iris. On flapper it is the beak tip. On mechtron it is
the visor strip. On mule it is a cargo strap buckle sitting on the back.
Zero gold is allowed for creatures where a highlight would distract; I
choose to use one on each because a small warm spark carries a lot of
character at badge size.

What will make these read at 18-32 px:

- Silhouettes are top-heavy or dominant-primitive: one big shape carries
  the identity, and the appendages hang off that anchor.
- Frame 2 alters the OUTLINE in a big way (limb angles, body twist, tuck).
- Gold shows up as a single 12-20 px focal dot -- an eye-hook.
- The double-outline (40-wide halo, 20-wide ink, body between) does the
  visibility lifting; the design just has to survive that stack.

Coverage: measured per creature after render. Expected 60-80 percent given
the fat strokes; numbers noted per creature in the SVG's hypothesis comment.

## The nine

### Humanoid -- the plucky everyman

Round helmet head over a sturdy jerkin, arms swinging honestly, feet planted
shoulder-width. Frame 1 is at attention, arms at sides; frame 2 mid-stride,
one leg lifted high, arms counter-swinging. Personality via wide surprised
eyes above a small smile -- the confident kind of plucky that expects the
job to work. Gold: a belt buckle at the waist. Only cast member that is a
person; the anchor everyone else diverges from.

### Flapper -- the imperious sky-hunter

A heraldic-eagle body: long horizontal wingspan in frame 1, small angular
beaked head, a triangular tail dropping below. Frame 2 tucks the wings
vertical for a dive -- the outline goes from wide-and-flat to tall-and-narrow
in one beat. Sharp forward-set eyes. Gold: the beak tip, a small triangle
of warm. Never touches ground; body hovers at mid-canvas so real clear air
sits beneath.

### Bonzoid -- the manic little machine

Johnny 5 as a picture-book toy. A wide tread rectangle at the bottom carries
a rounded dome body; oversized arms fly up like the machine is cheering. Two
slit eyes and an eyebrow arch of black; a beam-lamp glows on the chest.
Frame 2 flings the arms wider and tilts the body -- energy jumping out of the
frame. Gold: the chest lamp. Ground contact reads as one solid tread band --
that is the key silhouette split from mechtron, whose base has a hole.

### Gollumer -- the mournful oozing lump

A lopsided teardrop hunched forward, one hip bulging wider than the other,
a droopy neck-lump leaning off to one side, and a broad flat underside
touching everywhere it sits. Frame 1 leans right; frame 2 leans left with
the whole mass elongated forward -- the crown apex travels a good 60 units.
One big mournful eye with a gold iris (the round-1 finding: gold as iris
survives 18 px as a warm dot). Ground contact: the whole underside, flat.

### Spheroid -- the wide-eyed floating tripod

A huge orb dominating the upper canvas, three narrow legs dangling and never
reaching the ground -- clear air always below the leg tips. Frame 1: legs
hang straight; frame 2: legs kick to different angles and the orb tilts,
selling float, not walk. Two enormous wide surprised eyes with tiny pupils
plus a small "o" mouth of shock -- the startled-curious axis of the cast.
Gold: a small dome light on the forehead.

### Leggite -- the sly skittering column

A tall vertical stack of body-lobes with legs fringing both sides, a small
head with slit eyes on top. Frame 1 undulates one way (left-lobes push out
low, right-lobes push out high); frame 2 inverts the wave. Every leg on
frame 2 tilts the opposite direction of frame 1 -- the phase flip is the
motion. Ground: the many-legged bottom fringe. Gold: a small jewel on the
forehead.

### Mechtron -- the deadpan heavy machine

A rigid rectilinear slab body on two thick posts with a real black gap
between them -- the anti-bonzoid signal. Small block head, short shoulder
nubs (not swinging arms), a thin visor slit across the head with a gold
strip lit inside. Frame 2 lifts one post off the ground and tilts the whole
slab forward -- ponderous, not agile. Personality: a slot machine that has
seen it all. Gold: the visor strip.

### Packer -- the dopey cheerful hop

One huge egg-shaped body with a wide grinning mouth showing a gold tongue,
four tiny stubby T-rex feet planted just clear of the ground under the
belly. Frame 1 stands; frame 2 is airborne -- all four feet tucked up
against the belly, body squashed slightly wider and higher. Big round eyes
sitting above the grin. Ground silhouette in frame 1: belly-clear-with-four-
stubs, distinct from gollumer's all-flat and from mechtron's split-post.

### Mule -- the working machine

A horizontal chassis body on the wide 320x200 canvas; four thin post legs
with real daylight between them; a small block head on the right end with
two long ears; a stubby robot tail on the left. A cargo bin sits on the
back. Frame 1: legs planted, at rest. Frame 2: front-left and back-right
legs swing forward while the other diagonal swings back -- an honest trot
cycle. Gold: a cargo strap buckle on the top of the bin (no face on the
mule, per contract). Reads as a mule in silhouette (long ears, muzzle, long
horizontal body, tail) and as an industrial machine (rigid segments, thin
posts, exposed joints).
