# Cast 4, artist_3 interpretations

## Design hypothesis (cast level)

Persona: Pell Karo, a maker of hard-enamel lapel pins and die-cut vinyl
stickers. Influences: hard-enamel pins, cut-vinyl stickers, and mid-century
transit and park pictograms (the signage that has to read from across a
platform). Geometric vocabulary: every creature is ONE bold pictogram
silhouette, die-cut clean. The contract's mandatory white halo and dark
keyline are read here as the pin's polished metal rim, and the flat
`currentColor` body is read as poured enamel, so the whole cast looks like a
sheet of nine enamel pins.

I bet the read at 18 to 32 px on three things and almost nothing else: a
distinctive whole-body gesture, a purpose-built bottom edge (nine creatures,
nine different ground contacts), and one oversized enamel eye pair. Interiors
stay nearly empty so the rim and the eyes carry the shape. Machines
(mechtron, bonzoid, mule) are cut from hard rectangles; the everyman humanoid
and the soft creatures (flapper, spheroid, gollumer, packer, leggite) are cut
from curves, so an animal and a machine never share a body. Motion always
swings the whole pin, never a detail: every frame 2 changes the outline you
would see in the black shape. Gold enamel is spent at most once per pin, on a
single focal chip, and often not at all.

## The nine creatures

### Humanoid (walks, confident, plucky)

The everyman as a transit pictogram come to life: a round-headed biped with a
bell-shaped torso and a wide, chest-out stance. He is the friendly one, the
mascot, so he gets the only truly round head in the machine-and-animal cast
and a small gold badge on his chest like a pin worn on a pin. Frame 1 plants
both feet wide with a clear wedge of daylight between them. Frame 2 is an
unmistakable march: one leg lifts and swings forward while the other stays
planted, and one arm punches up, so the black shape reads as a stride and not
a stand. The wedge split at the feet is his signature bottom edge.

### Flapper (flies, sharp, imperious)

A large pterodactyl that treats the sky as its throne. It never touches down;
the whole pin floats in the upper canvas with open air beneath it. The read is
all in the wingspan and the sharp beaked head on a lifted neck, with a small
back crest for hauteur. Frame 1 holds the wings out wide and level in a glide;
frame 2 sweeps them down in a deep flap, so the silhouette visibly beats. The
one gold chip is the beak tip, a hard imperious point. Its bottom edge is
nothing at all: clear air, which together with the wings separates it cleanly
from the other never-lands creature, the orbed spheroid.

### Bonzoid (rolls on treads, manic, boisterous)

Johnny 5 as an over-caffeinated enamel pin: a boxy machine head and torso with
two oversized arms, riding a single fat rounded tread that sits flat on the
ground. It has no legs; the tread is the whole base and it is continuous, one
solid pill, which is exactly what separates it in silhouette from mechtron's
split posts. Frame 1 throws both big arms up; frame 2 tilts the body forward
and swaps the arms high and low so the pin looks like it is barreling ahead.
A wide manic visor and a gold chest bolt finish the read.

### Gollumer (slimes, mournful, gentle)

A soft lopsided slug in the Hutt sense: one continuous oozing mound that meets
the ground along its entire flat underside, with no break anywhere. It leans
to one side on purpose; asymmetry is its identity. The face is the whole point:
big heavy-lidded eyes with low pupils and a downturned mouth, a gentle sad
giant. Frame 1 sits and settles; frame 2 stretches the mass forward and pushes
a bulge of slime off the leading edge, so the ooze is legible in the outline.
It carries the cast's one optional under-shade, a low dark band, to give the
soft body weight. Its unbroken flat base is the opposite of packer's four-footed
belly gap.

### Spheroid (floats, startled, wide-eyed)

A War-of-the-Worlds saucer with a cockpit dome and three thin tentacle legs
dangling beneath it that never, in either frame, reach the ground. It hovers
high with clear air under the leg tips. The face is pure alarm: two huge white
eyes with tiny pupils and a small round open mouth, a machine perpetually
surprised to be seen. A single gold beacon sits on the dome. Frame 1 hangs the
legs straight; frame 2 bobs the orb up and sways the legs to one side, so the
float reads as a live hover, not a parked disc. Orb-plus-tentacles is its
separation from the winged flapper.

### Leggite (slithers on many legs, sly, skittering)

A tall narrow column that undulates like a standing centipede, fringed with
many small legs down both sides and a row of little feet at its base. It is the
schemer of the cast: narrow slanted eyes and a down-angled brow give it a sly,
sidelong look. Its S-curved column and its many-legged bottom edge are unlike
anything else in the lineup. Frame 1 curves the column one way; frame 2 mirrors
the curve and shifts the leg phase, so the whole tower appears to skitter
sideways. Asymmetry is deliberate and identity-bearing here.

### Mechtron (walks heavy, stoic, deadpan)

A heavy rectilinear machine with no curves to spare: a blocky torso and head,
stubby thick arms, and two thick posts for legs with a wide rectangular hole
between them. The hole is the signature; it is what tells mechtron from bonzoid
in the black shape, split base against solid tread. The face is a single level
visor with two evenly spaced dark slots, flat and deadpan, no expression at all.
Frame 1 stands square; frame 2 plants one post and lifts the other into a short,
ponderous step while the body sinks a little under its own weight. A gold chest
square is its one bright chip.

### Packer (hops, dopey, cheerful)

A huge rounded monster that is its own cargo, balanced on four comically tiny
legs, with the belly clearly arched clear of the ground between the four little
feet. It is the happy dope of the cast: two big goofy eyes and an enormous grin
with a single gold tooth. Frame 1 crouches with all four feet down; frame 2 is
a full toad hop, the whole mass lifted and the tiny legs splayed and tucked off
the ground, so nothing touches the floor and the hop is unmistakable. Four feet
with a belly gap is its bottom edge, the deliberate opposite of gollumer's one
unbroken base.

### Mule (plods, quietly dependable)

A literal robotic pack mule on the wide canvas, built like BigDog: a long
segmented horizontal chassis, a front shoulder block and a rear haunch block, a
lifted neck ending in long ears and a blunt muzzle, a short tail, and four thin
leg columns holding the slab well clear of the ground with real daylight
beneath. No face, no head crop, as the contract requires; it is a working
machine, not a pet. One gold hazard strip on the flank is its only bright mark.
Frame 1 stands with legs staggered; frame 2 swings the diagonal leg pairs
through a steady plod. It reads as a mule in silhouette by the ears, muzzle,
horizontal body, and tail, and as a machine by its rigid segmented build.
